What is AI visibility and why your business needs it

March 22, 2026 · 5 min read

AI visibility is simple: it's whether AI tools recommend your business when a potential customer asks for your services. If someone opens ChatGPT and asks "who's the best plumber in Raleigh," does your name come up? If not, you have an AI visibility problem.

And it matters more than most business owners realize.

The shift from Google to AI

For 20 years, Google was the only game in town. You paid for ads, optimized your SEO, and showed up in search results. That system still works today, but it's no longer the only way people find businesses.

ChatGPT now handles 2 billion queries per day. It captured 17% of the global search market in early 2026, making it the first real competitor to Google in over two decades. Three out of four Americans are already using AI to search weekly. Among younger demographics, the shift is even more dramatic: 70% of Gen Z and Millennials are using AI search tools regularly.

Gartner predicts that traditional search volume will drop 25% as users shift to AI. The AI search market is projected to capture over 60% of total search volume by 2030. This isn't a trend. It's a structural shift in how people find services.

Why AI visibility is different from SEO

When someone searches Google, they see 10 results and pick one. When someone asks AI, they get one or two recommendations. That's it. There's no page two. There's no scrolling. AI either mentions your business or it doesn't.

This makes AI visibility a winner-take-all situation. The businesses AI recommends get the customer. Everyone else gets nothing. And unlike Google, where you can pay for an ad to appear at the top, you can't buy your way into an AI recommendation. You have to earn it.

The good news: AI search traffic converts at 14.2% compared to Google's 2.8%. When AI recommends you, the customer is 5x more likely to actually hire you. The traffic is dramatically more valuable.

What happens if you ignore AI visibility

Every month you're not visible to AI, potential customers are asking for exactly the services you offer and being sent to your competitors. They never visit your website. They never see your Google ad. They never call you. They just go to whoever AI recommended.

For a plumber in a mid-size city, that could be 20-40 lost leads per month. For a roofer, where a single job is worth $8,000-15,000, even one lost customer per month is a significant hit to revenue.

The businesses that set up their AI visibility now are going to lock in their position before competitors catch on. This is SEO in 2010 all over again. The early movers won then, and the early movers will win now.

How to check your AI visibility

The simplest test: open ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini and ask "who's the best [your service] in [your city]?" If your business doesn't come up, you have work to do.
